Questions; and their answers

In academia, people at times encounter research happening in various different frontiers. Specialists of similar fields, working at their own respective frontiers, are able to communicate with surprising ease and efficiency. Naturally, knowing and understanding complicated words and their scientific or mathematical meaning is important. But it is commonplace, as a newcomer in a research domain, to be lost in the jargon. I begin the documentation of a sequence of questions and their answers in order to understand the flow of ideas shaping the motivation and background of my research work.

The first set of questions is a dialogue between a school kid and a PhD student. So, the stage is set with a curious troublemaker and an unemployed youth in disguise.
